Why this model
The GP is statistically optimal for the 355-sample WindsorML dataset. Unlike any other model, it predicts a complete probability distribution — every prediction carries a calibrated confidence interval. In densely sampled regions, GP uncertainty collapses to near zero. In sparse regions it inflates, acting as a direct signal for where the next CFD simulation should be placed (Bayesian optimisation). Uses a Matern(ν=2.5) kernel — twice-differentiable, appropriate for smooth aerodynamic surfaces.
Sample size context
355 samples — optimal GP range 500+ — Neural Network begins to compete 1000+ — GP matrix inversion becomes costly

Why this model
Gradient Boosting trains trees sequentially, with each tree fitting the residuals of all previous ones. A learning rate of 0.05 forces many small correction steps, preventing overfitting. Shallow trees (max depth 3) capture dominant nonlinear relationships without memorising noise. The recommended model for a single definitive Cd estimate in a design review — consistent and deterministic.

Why this model
The MLP is included as a scaling benchmark. At 355 samples it is the weakest performer; the GP's Bayesian kernel formulation is better suited to smooth aerodynamic surfaces at this count. At 500+ DrivAerML samples the MLP is projected to reach RMSE near 0.012, approaching the GP. At 1000+ it becomes the preferred architecture. Why a stacked ensemble
No single model is optimal across every region of the Windsor Cd surface. Tree ensembles capture sharp nonlinearities near critical fastback separation thresholds; the GP captures the smooth interior with calibrated uncertainty. Stacking lets each learner contribute where strongest.
Weight transparency
At 355 samples the stack is GP-dominant (96.9%). XGB, LGB and CatBoost receive zero weight because GP dominates at this sample count. As the dataset grows toward 1000+ samples, weights will redistribute toward gradient-boosted trees.
Configuration
Base learners: XGBoost, LightGBM, CatBoost, ExtraTrees, GP(Matern ν=2.5) Feature engineering: pairwise products, x², log(x), √x, dynamic pressure q Target transform: log(Cd) — stabilises variance Out-of-fold: 5-fold CV, 5-seed bagging per base learner Meta-combiner: hill-climb vs positive-Ridge, winner by inner CV
Results on WindsorML
CV ranking: Custom 0.00779, GP 0.00946, RF 0.01418, GB 0.01624, MLP 0.01850. Custom stack beats the best single baseline by 17.6% in RMSE and 13.3% in MAE.

Why this approach reduces CFD simulation count
Traditional aerodynamic design requires running a CFD simulation for every design variant — 6 to 48 hours per run on a high-performance cluster. The surrogate model approach breaks this barrier. The 355 WindsorML WMLES runs train a mathematical model that approximates the full CFD solver. Once trained, the surrogate predicts Cd in under one millisecond for any Windsor parameter combination, enabling rapid screening of thousands of design variants. The Gaussian Process additionally provides uncertainty estimates enabling active learning — the next CFD simulation is always placed at the point of highest model uncertainty. This Bayesian optimisation strategy can reduce the simulation count required to find an optimal design by five to ten times compared to random or grid sampling.
WindsorML Parameter Definitions
The 7 dimensionless Windsor body geometry parameters used as model inputs
ratio_length_back_fast (rlbf)
Ratio of rear fastback length to total body length. Lower values = steeper rear end with earlier separation. Higher values = longer gradual taper with delayed separation.
ratio_height_nose_windshield (rhnw)
Height ratio between the nose leading edge and windshield base. Controls how bluff the front face appears to oncoming flow and how aggressively streamlines transition to the windshield plane.
ratio_height_fast_back (rhfb)
Height ratio of the fastback surface relative to overall body height. Governs the severity of rear slope angle and the onset of trailing-edge separation from the roof surface.
side_taper
Side wall taper angle in degrees. Positive taper narrows the Windsor body toward the rear, reducing the width of the separated wake and typically lowering total drag.
clearance
Ground clearance in millimetres. Affects underbody flow velocity and the balance between base pressure recovery and underbody suction force.
bottom_taper_angle
Underbody diffuser ramp angle in degrees. Higher angles increase pressure recovery but risk diffuser stall above approximately 12 degrees, which reverses the drag benefit.
frontal_area (m²)
Projected frontal area at Windsor body scale. Directly scales the pressure drag component: Cd = Fd / (0.5 × ρ × V² × A). Windsor body values range from approximately 0.080 to 0.120 m².
